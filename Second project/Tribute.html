Mother Teresa

1910-1997

"A Life Not Lived For Others Is Not A Life..."

Biography:

Mother Teresa, born Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, in Skopje (modern-day North Macedonia), was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary known for her selfless service to the poor and destitute. At the age of 18, she joined the Loreto Sisters in Ireland and later moved to India, where she dedicated her life to helping those in need. In 1950, she founded the Missionaries of Charity, an organization devoted to caring for "the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind, the lepers," and all those suffering from neglect and poverty. Her work began in the slums of Calcutta (now Kolkata) and expanded globally, with over 4,500 nuns operating in more than 130 countries. Her unwavering compassion and devotion earned her numerous accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 and India's Bharat Ratna in 1980. Despite facing criticism for her views on certain social issues, she remained steadfast in her mission of love and service. Mother Teresa passed away on September 5, 1997, but her legacy continues to inspire millions. She was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by Pope Francis in 2016.

Mother Teresa's Life:

  • 1910: Born on August 26 as Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu in Skopje, Ottoman Empire (modern-day North Macedonia).
  • 1919: Father, Nikollë Bojaxhiu, dies, leaving the family in financial difficulty.
  • 1928: At the age of 18, leaves home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland.
  • 1929: Arrives in Calcutta, India, to begin her novitiate with the Loreto order.
  • 1931: Takes her first religious vows and adopts the name Teresa, after St. Thérèse of Lisieux.
  • 1937: Takes her final vows, becoming "the spouse of Jesus for all eternity."
  • 1946: Experiences a "call within a call" during a train journey to Darjeeling, inspiring her to leave the convent and serve the poorest of the poor.
  • 1948: Receives permission from the Vatican to leave the Loreto convent and begins her independent mission in Calcutta. Adopts a white sari with a blue border as her habit. Opens her first school in the slums of Calcutta.
  • 1950: Founds the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation dedicated to serving the poor and destitute.
  • 1955: Establishes Nirmala Shishu Bhavan, a home for orphaned and abandoned children.
  • 1962: Receives her first major recognition, the Padma Shri award from the Government of India.
  • 1997: Steps down as the head of the Missionaries of Charity in March.Dies on September 5 in Calcutta at the age of 87.

Awards Received by Mother Teresa:

  • 1962: Padma Shri (India's fourth highest civilian award)
  • 1962: Ramon Magsaysay Award for Peace and International Understanding
  • 1971: Pope John XXIII Peace Prize
  • 1972: Jawaharlal Nehru Award for International Understanding
  • 1979: Nobel Peace Prize for her humanitarian work and dedication to the poor
  • 1980: Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian award)
  • 1983: Order of Merit (United Kingdom)
  • 1985: Presidential Medal of Freedom (United States)
  • 1996: Honorary Citizenship of the United States
  • 2003: Beatified as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta" by Pope John Paul II
  • 2016: Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by Pope Francis
